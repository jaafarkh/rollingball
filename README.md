# Motion on a curved surface
this repository provide a simple yet poweful simulator to show the motion of a small ball on a concave curved surface.
This simulator gives good insight into the constant energy principle of mechanical systems. In the absence of losses, 
the sum of Kinetic Energy (KE) and Potential Energy (PE) remain fixed.
Consider a frictionless rolling starting at the top of a hill:
At the top: 
  Kinetic Energy is 0, and Potential Energy is at its maximum 100%.
  
At the bottom:
  Potential Energy is 0, and Kinetic Energy is at its maximum 100%.
  
The sum: (PE + KE) equals the exact same number at every single point on the track.

In the real world, no system is perfectly efficient. As energy transfers or transforms, 
some portion of it is inevitably converted into unusable forms, 
most commonly random thermal energy (heat), sound, or vibration.
Accounting for non-conservative forces like friction:
  PE1 + KE1 = PE2 + KE2 + Lost Energy
